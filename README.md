## 📚 Learning Philosophy

> "I don't claim expertise—I claim consistent effort."

My approach to cybersecurity is rooted in **methodical practice** and **evidence-based learning**. Every challenge I solve teaches me not just what works, but why it works. Every failed exploit attempt is a lesson in system behavior.

**Core principles that guide my learning:**

**Process over results**  
Understanding the methodology behind an attack is more valuable than just capturing a flag. I document my thought process, failed attempts, and successful exploits to build a reproducible framework.

**Verify everything**  
Never trust without evidence. Every claim needs technical proof—HTTP responses, logs, network captures, or source code. This discipline prevents assumptions from becoming facts.

**Build to break**  
Creating vulnerable applications and CTF challenges has taught me more about security than exploiting them. When you understand how systems are built, you see where they're weak.

**Learn in public**  
Sharing writeups, documenting tools, and contributing to security communities isn't just about giving back—it's about learning through teaching. Explaining a concept solidifies understanding.

**Embrace failure**  
Every unsolved CTF challenge, every failed exploit, every misconfigured tool is data. Debugging teaches system behavior. Getting stuck forces creative thinking. Mistakes reveal blind spots.
